Why This Site Exists

Corsair Standard checks formatting mechanics, not whether a person is a good candidate.

The Problem

Resume formatting can look correct on the surface while being fragile underneath. A date may be pushed across the page with spaces. A section divider may be a typed line instead of a paragraph border. A bullet may look aligned until one word changes and the whole row shifts.

Those problems are hard to see in a PDF screenshot, but they matter when a resume is edited, reviewed, copied, or converted.

What Corsair Standard Checks

  • Page margins, section order, section headings, and divider rules.
  • Right-aligned dates built with tab stops instead of space padding.
  • Bullet indentation, blank-line rhythm, and paragraph spacing.
  • Font family, font size, bold, italic, and visual hierarchy consistency.
  • Structural risks such as tables, text boxes, manual alignment, and unstable spacing.

What It Does Not Check

Corsair Standard does not grade experience, rank applicants, predict recruiting outcomes, or decide whether a resume is good enough. It is deliberately narrow: the app checks whether the Word document is built cleanly.

Why That Narrowness Matters

A focused formatting audit is more useful than a vague resume score. When the app flags something, it should point to a concrete Word behavior you can fix: tabs, margins, spacing, comments, section labels, or typography.
