Formatting Guide

How the clean document is built, and why those choices make resumes easier to maintain.

Use Tab Stops For Dates

The clean template uses saved right tab stops for dates. That means the date aligns to a stable position on the ruler instead of being pushed over with spaces. If the role title changes, the date stays anchored.

Avoid Spacebar Alignment

Repeated spaces are fragile because every font, screen, printer, and edit can shift them. Use Word paragraph alignment, indentation, and tabs instead.

Keep Bullets Mechanically Consistent

First-level bullets should share the same bullet position and text indent. Sub-bullets can be indented further, but they should be intentional and consistent across the document.

Use Paragraph Spacing, Not Blank Paragraphs

Extra blank lines are easy to create and hard to maintain. The clean template relies on paragraph spacing so the document keeps a predictable rhythm.

Keep The Visual Hierarchy Simple

  • Section labels are uppercase and bold.
  • Organizations are bold where appropriate.
  • Roles are italicized where appropriate.
  • Body text uses a consistent font family and size.

Start From The Template When Structure Is Noisy

If an audit produces a large number of issues, the fastest fix is often not line-by-line repair. Move the content into the clean template, preserve the intended wording, and let the template carry the alignment and spacing rules.
